Key terms

SKU database — the single shared product database that feeds both internal operations and the SME portal. Currently updated by hand.
SME portal — Compass's digital storefront where business customers order at contract pricing, drawing from the same SKU database.
PIM — Product Information Management system. Compass tried one and it failed — on the workflow, not the data.
Rule store — the published, versioned set of per-jurisdiction labelling rules + brand guidelines the engine writes against and checks against.
Completeness checklist — the machine-readable list of what a complete product entry must contain; the system chases whatever is missing.
Review Coordinator — the single human role owning routing, queues, supplier chasing and approval config. At Compass: the supplier-management / digital-commerce lead.
Sandbox vs governed — sandbox = private draft generation & scoring (not official); governed = the recorded, committing process from review onward.
Label Rouge / organic — example certified claims that need a valid, current certificate before they can be published.
Jurisdiction-specific copy — country-tailored product descriptions, not just translation. Whether Compass needs this is unconfirmed and flagged at B3.
P26 — the new pattern minted for this case: rule-bounded multi-variant content generation — the engine's first generative step.

The iteration loops

Where the process cycles back. Each loop is a real workflow object (P21), not an email thread — held in the system until resolved.
1 · Supplier chaseC2 / C3 ↔ A2 / B1
The item is blocked until the requested document or data arrives, then re-enters enrichment automatically. Replaces the liaison-driven email loop that broke when someone was absent.
2 · Legal amend (governed)D1 → B3
A regulatory amend triggers re-evaluation (P21 material edit); rule-matching re-runs with the new evidentiary citation. Stays inside the governed track.
3 · Brand rejectD2 → B2
A reject sends the variant back to re-enrichment (P26 generation) with the reviewer's note carried in as a hard constraint.
Role note: P18–P21 are owned by one Review Coordinator — at Compass, the supplier-management / digital-commerce lead. That single role plus the cockpit (P20) is the concrete answer to “when a liaison is absent it's a mess”: routing, queues and chase-state live in the system, not in the absent person.
Governance note: the B-phase runs advisory under P14 sandbox; the regime flips to governed / committing at D2 sign-off; P15 Confirm / Override / Query sits on every AI artefact across A–F — the justifiable-reason backbone for every human row.
New pattern: P26 Rule-bounded multi-variant content generation (B2, E1) — the engine's first generative pattern; it produces the copy, where P12–P25 evaluate it. Generated spans are grounded + cited, then handed straight to P12 / P16 / P22.

Design decision inventory — why the process looks this way
The decisions that shaped the process above, each with its rationale, build implication, and source. [MN] / [PS] = canonical · [PL] = pattern library · [O] = our design · [?] = unconfirmed.
# Design decision Rationale Implication for the build Source
D1 The spine is orchestration, not storageLead with the workflow layer (chase / queue / cockpit / audit), not a better database. Compass's prior PIM failed on the workflow — absent liaisons, products bouncing back to start, missing documents — not on the data model. Phase C (Orchestrate & Chase) and phase F (Audit) carry the weight; a PIM stays in place beside the system. [MN L53] · [O]
D2 One actor per row; humans only where justifiedLegal accountability, externally-held input, binding decision, or brand taste. Human-in-the-loop must have a reason (P15) — not capability. The AI does the reversible compute; humans own what's official. AI acts at A2–B6, E1–F2; humans act at S2, A1, C3, C4, D1, D2. No human step without a stated justification. [PL §2.4 / P15]
D3 Workflow is human-owned BPM, not AIP18–P21 consolidated under a single Review Coordinator. The library is explicit: workflow patterns are deterministic and AI-augmented, owned by one coordinating role — that is what makes them coherent. A cockpit (P20) + assignment (P18) + clarification lifecycle (P21), not an “AI does the chasing” black box. [PL §2.4] · [MN L53]
D4 Generation is a new pattern (P26); evaluation is reusedThe library was an evaluation engine; ProductCopyOS adds production. Writing brand-compliant copy is not covered by P01–P25 (P17 packages existing facts, it does not generate copy). It is genuinely net-new. B2 / E1 generate under rule + brand constraints, then hand straight to P12 / P16 / P22 to be evaluated — never trusted on generation confidence. [O] · [PS §3.3 / 3.6]
D5 Two-desk gate (Legal → Brand), governed regimeNon-binding regulatory review, then binding brand approval. Legal drives core claim vocabulary; Brand owns tone and positioning (Product Spec A4 / A5). Both sign off before publish. D1 non-binding review (P19 review phase); D2 binding approval (P19 approval phase); P15 confirm / override throughout. [PS §3.4] · [PL P19]
D6 Leave B3 jurisdiction-copy depth unconfirmedLoad-bearing assumption for the whole pitch. If the SME portal is translation-only, B3 narrows to translation + compliance and P26 has little per-jurisdiction work; if country-specific copy exists, the full case holds. Rule-matching scope at B3 is not locked until Mhamed confirms; the artefact flags it rather than assuming. [MN L11 / L65] · [?]

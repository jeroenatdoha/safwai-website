The complication · what breaks today

The claim is right once, on paper. Then it degrades every time a human re-keys it.

VitaPet's claims, legal text and rationing are written once, correctly. They then get copied by hand across FR, DE and US, drifting between the packaging tool and the PIM. Translation and manual copy-paste is the number-one time sink on this account.
Failure 1 · re-keyed by hand
Translated, not transferred
Claims and legal text are re-typed market by market instead of flowing through. Every re-key is a chance for the regulated wording to drift.
Failure 2 · desync
Packaging tool and PIM drift apart
A new version drops and the packaging tool and the PIM fall out of sync. The legal claims are not controlled and become inaccurate.
Failure 3 · the blind spot
Filled is not compliant
The PIM checks that a field is filled, never that the value is compliant. An inaccurate therapeutic claim clears the check and reaches the live site.
Your PIM confirms the field is filled. It never confirms the claim is still true after it has been translated and re-typed across three markets. On a veterinary diet, that gap is a market withdrawal.
This is the tax we remove. Screen 2 is where we close it.Framing 2 / 4

Glossary

The terms on the glass

Every regulated or technical term used in this walk-through, in plain language.
Claim parity The same regulated claim must mean exactly the same thing in FR, DE and US. Terminology is fixed, never creatively varied, so translation cannot drift the claim.
Nutrition-vs-medicinal line The hard boundary of the category. A feed may support a function, never diagnose, treat or cure. Every vet claim is held against it.
Product passport (PLM) The master and local product record that acts as the layer where claims originate. The engine feeds validated claims back into it, upstream.
PIM Product Information Management system. Holds product data and checks completeness; it does not check whether a claim is valid.
PARNUTs "Particular nutritional purposes", the EU intended-use wording for veterinary diets, Reg. (EU) 2020/354. The EU side of the FR and DE variants.
FEDIAF and 767/2009 The EU pet-food nutritional and labelling code, and the feed-labelling regulation. The core EU rule set; 1831/2003 governs additives.
AAFCO and FDA-CVM US model regulations, nutrient profiles and state feed registration. The US regime, a real contrast with EU PARNUTs.
Analytical constituents The declared nutritional values (protein, phosphorus and so on). Precise terms that must translate identically and pair with each claim.
MSC Marine Stewardship Council certification for fish-sourcing claims. A public register with an expiry cycle, the live-validation case here.
FSSC 22000 A food-safety certification scheme for manufacturing. Checked alongside MSC and any market ecolabels.
Self-substantiation Where a claim is not on a regulatory list, the brand backs it with its own internal studies. The engine surfaces unsubstantiated claims for review, never generates around them.
Claims spreadsheet The internal Excel where VitaPet's claims originate (SKU, territory, claim text, constituents, rationing, legal text). There is no supplier feed.
Two desks Regulatory and Compliance (Dr. Fontaine, the lead gate, core wording and the medicinal line) and Brand and Portfolio (Marc Devlin, the expression). Regulatory leads on vet copy.
DPP Digital Product Passport. For pet food it is framed here as "coming", a sustainability and MSC adjacency off the same audit trail, not a dated obligation.

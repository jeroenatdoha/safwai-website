Full Process Table — from the internal claims spreadsheet to a validated, audited product passport

The claim-parity variant. VitaPet ingests no supplier feed — product data originates internally in a claims spreadsheet, and the engine feeds validated claims back into the product passport (the PLM layer where claims originate). Its headline pain is translation: claims, legal text and rationing are re-keyed by hand across FR / DE / US and drift between the packaging tool and the PIM. So the spine foregrounds a claim-parity lock (regulated wording identical-in-substance across languages), the nutrition-vs-medicinal boundary, and live MSC validation for marine claims. One SKU family carries two audiences: a veterinary renal diet and a breed-specific retail line.

Key terms

Claims spreadsheet — the internal source-of-truth Excel where product claims originate (SKU, claim territory, claim text, analytical constituents, rationing, legal text). There is no supplier feed.
Product passport (PLM) — the master / local product record that acts as the PLM layer where claims live. The engine feeds validated claims back into it — the architecture money-point.
Claim parity — the same regulated claim must mean exactly the same thing in FR, DE and US; terminology is fixed, never creatively varied. Translation must not drift the claim.
Nutrition-vs-medicinal line — the hard boundary: a feed may support function, never diagnose, treat or cure. The guardrail every vet claim is held against.
PARNUTs — “particular nutritional purposes” — the EU intended-use wording for veterinary diets (Reg. 2020/354). The EU side of the FR/DE cards.
FEDIAF / 767/2009 — the EU pet-food nutritional & labelling code and feed-labelling regulation. The core EU rule set; 1831/2003 governs additives.
AAFCO / FDA-CVM — US model regulations, nutrient profiles and state feed control. The US card’s regime — a real contrast with EU PARNUTs.
Analytical constituents — the declared nutritional values (protein, phosphorus, etc.). Precise terms that must translate identically and pair with each benefit.
MSC — Marine Stewardship Council certification for fish-based sourcing claims. Public API with an expiry cycle — the live-validation demo here.
Self-substantiation — where a claim is not explicitly listed in the framework, the brand backs it with its own internal studies. The engine surfaces unsubstantiated claims for review, never generates around them.

The iteration loops

Where the process cycles back. Each loop is a real workflow object (P21), not an email thread — held in the system until resolved.
1 · Substantiation loopC1 / C2 / C3 ↔ B4
An unsubstantiated claim is held until internal evidence / a study arrives, then re-validates automatically. Surfaced for review, never generated around.
2 · Parity driftB3 → C1 · D1 → B3
A translation that drifts from the locked claim re-enters the parity check and re-validates; a regulatory amend re-runs parity with the new citation. This is the loop that kills the re-keying.
3 · Brand rejectD2 → B1 / B2
An expression reject sends the variant back to re-generation with the reviewer’s note carried in as a hard constraint.
4 · PLM sync (upstream)E2 → product passport
Validated claims flow back upstream into the product passport where claims originate — closing the packaging-tool ↔ PIM desync that forced the hand re-keying.
The wedge: translation is the #1 time sink — claims, legal text and rationing re-keyed by hand across FR/DE/US, drifting between the packaging tool and the PIM. The claim-parity lock (B3) holds regulated wording identical-in-substance across markets; the PLM feedback (E2) feeds validated claims back into the product passport where they originate. Together they remove the re-keying and the desync.
Two desks, regulatory-led: Regulatory/Compliance (D1) owns the core claim wording and the nutrition-vs-medicinal line; Brand/Portfolio (D2) owns the expression. Claim sensitivity makes the regulatory desk the lead gate; P15 Confirm / Override / Query sits on every AI artefact.
Guardrail: the nutrition-vs-medicinal line is hard — no diagnosis, treatment or cure; unsubstantiated claims are surfaced for review, never generated around. New pattern: P27 live external-authority validation (MSC). Reused: P26 multi-variant generation; claim-parity is a specialisation — P26 canonical-claim-lock + a P22 cross-language consistency category — not a new pattern. DPP: framed as “coming” (MSC / sustainability adjacency), not a deadline.
Commercial frame: PoC ≈ €60–80k, transformation-program funded; success = claims / legal text validated FR/DE/US flowing into the PIM + product passport with an audit trail, cutting the manual copy/paste; data-residency + security review heavy. [YI B.10]

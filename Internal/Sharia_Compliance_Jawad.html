Two processes · Two distinct problems

Where the system intervenes.

Each module targets a different operational process: a different set of people, a different failure mode, a different bottleneck to remove.

A
Inquiry Resolution
Retail & private banking · front-line staff
Process
A customer or front-line officer asks whether a product, transaction, or activity is Shariah-permissible. The question needs an answer: cited and consistent.
Pain
Different officers give different answers to the same question. Routine inquiries queue behind complex ones on the advisor's desk. No audit trail. Response quality depends on who picks up.
Solution
AI retrieves the applicable Board ruling and AAOIFI standard on submission. The Shariah Desk Officer reviews and endorses, or flags for escalation. Novel cases reach the Internal Shariah Advisor pre-framed.
Value
Every inquiry answered the same way, every time, with a citation. The advisor handles only what requires genuine judgment.
B
Commercial Financing Clearance
SME & corporate financing · Relationship Managers
Process
A Relationship Manager submits an SME or corporate financing application. Before proceeding to credit, the deal must pass Shariah clearance across eight aspects.
Pain
Clearance takes 3–14 days. The same sectors and product structures are reviewed from scratch each time. Document gaps surface mid-process. Deals stall while the client waits, or walks.
Solution
Eight aspects screened in parallel on receipt. Document requirements named on Day 1. Precedent-covered cases cleared at Shariah Desk Officer level, same morning. Complex cases escalate with full reasoning attached.
Value
Most deals cleared the morning they are submitted. The Relationship Manager calls the client with a decision. Before the competing bank does.

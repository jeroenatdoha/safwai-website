Process Swimlanes — one diagram per phase (S–F)

Horizontal swimlanes (rows = roles). Each phase opens and closes on named state / deliverable circles (a phase may have several of either); process steps are rectangles in the acting role’s lane, each named verb + noun; every arrow is annotated with the specific deliverable or information it carries. System (AI) steps also carry their pattern numbers (from the master Pattern Library) beneath the box. The claim-parity variant: claims originate internally (no supplier feed), the headline step is B3 claim-parity lock, and validated claims are fed back upstream into the product passport. State / deliverable (named; ≥1 per side) Process step (verb + noun) arrow = deliverable / info external system pattern №s under System steps Lanes: System · Regulatory & Compliance Lead · Claims / Scientific owner (internal) · Brand / Portfolio
Roles (lanes): the actors in the process table — System (the tool, [O]), Regulatory & Compliance Lead (S2, D1) and Brand / Portfolio (D2, named in Yassine input B.1), plus the internal Claims / Regulatory owner (claims origin, A1) and Regulatory & Scientific Affairs (evidence in the chase loop, C2). VitaPet has no supplier lane — claims originate internally, so the green lane is the internal claims / scientific owner, not a supplier feed. External systems (MSC register, PIM, product passport / PLM, channels) are dashed boxes, not lanes.
Multiple states: phase B closes on two (clean → D · flags → C); phase C opens on three blockers (unsubstantiated claim · parity drift · expired cert); phase D closes on three (approve · amend · reject); phase F closes on two deliverables (audit pack · DPP payload, forward). Each phase’s end state(s) are the next phase’s start state(s).
The wedge: B3 translate with a claim-parity lock — the step that kills the hand re-keying across FR/DE/US — and E2 feeds validated claims back upstream into the product passport (PLM), closing the packaging-tool ↔ PIM desync. New patterns: none. P27 (live external-authority validation, MSC) is reused from the library; claim-parity is a specialisation of P26 (canonical-claim-lock) + a P22 cross-language consistency category, not a new pattern. DPP framed as “coming” (MSC / sustainability adjacency), not a registry-write deadline.

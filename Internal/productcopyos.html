ProductCopyOS · the problem we're solving

Brand judgment is getting eaten by data hygiene work.

Consumer brands sell into many markets. Each market needs its own product copy — legal claim wording, ingredient declarations, regulatory body fine print, all shaped to that jurisdiction's rules. The brand-side people who are supposed to be making senior brand judgments are instead spending their days filtering supplier-fed product data and rewriting it into launch-ready copy, market by market.

3–4 h
per day, per marketing manager
Manual filtering and reformatting of supplier-fed product data — before any brand or regulatory thinking happens. Pieter's pain at Maison Vellard NL & DACH.
3×
the work for every additional market
Each new jurisdiction means another claim-wording sweep, another regulatory check, another audit trail. The work scales linearly with markets and SKUs.
0
compliance audit trail by default
"Which rule applied to which variant, and who approved it?" lives in spreadsheets and email threads. The day a regulator asks, somebody opens their inbox.
Why it matters
The senior people aren't doing senior work.
Time-to-launch slips. Compliance becomes a person's job, not a system property. The brand judgment the brand manager was hired for is the lost output — squeezed out by the mechanical work that nobody else can do either.
The solution
ProductCopyOS — the assembly is automated; the judgment stays human.
We take the raw supplier feed and produce validated, jurisdiction-ready product copy — with the brand manager at the gate, never replaced by it. Every decision logged, every rule citable, every variant traceable to its source.

A2 · prominent
I’m assuming the brand’s copywriting guidelines live in SharePoint and we can read them clause-by-clause across the whole library.

What if they live in a vendor copy tool — or worse, in someone’s head?
1
The rules live where the brand already keeps them
Brand guidelines stay in SharePoint, in the brand’s own tenant. Jurisdictional rules stay in the authority’s published documents — DGCCRF, NVWA, BVL, EC 767/2009. No parallel CMS, no per-system rule database to keep in sync with two places.
When a brand-side editor amends a clause, the next validation run cites the new version — no engineering ticket.
2
Every citation traces back to a clause in a versioned source
Every BG-* and every FR-/NL-/DE-/EU- rule on Screen 2 is a link, not a label. Click it, and you land on the exact clause — version, last-edit, authority, in-force date all attached. The audit trail keeps the citation, not a summary of it.
Compliance can audit any decision back to the clause that justified it — without asking the system to remember.

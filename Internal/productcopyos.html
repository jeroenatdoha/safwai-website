ProductCopyOS · the problem we're solving

Brand judgment is getting eaten by data hygiene work.

Consumer brands sell into many markets. Each market needs its own product copy — legal claim wording, ingredient declarations, regulatory body fine print, all shaped to that jurisdiction's rules. The brand-side people who are supposed to be making senior brand judgments are instead rewriting supplier-fed product data into launch-ready copy and rule-checking it against each market — by hand, one variant at a time.

3–4 h
per day, per marketing manager
Sifting supplier feeds, rewriting into brand language, producing one variant per market, rule-checking each one — before any brand or regulatory thinking happens. Pieter's pain at Maison Vellard NL & DACH.
3×
the work for every additional market
Each new jurisdiction means another claim-wording sweep, another regulatory check, another audit trail. The work scales linearly with markets and SKUs.
0
compliance audit trail by default
"Which rule applied to which variant, and who approved it?" lives in spreadsheets and email threads. The day a regulator asks, somebody opens their inbox.
Why it matters
The senior people aren't doing senior work.
Time-to-launch slips. Compliance becomes a person's job, not a system property. The brand judgment the brand manager was hired for is the lost output — squeezed out by the mechanical work that nobody else can do either.
The solution
ProductCopyOS — the assembly is automated; the judgment stays human.
We take the raw supplier feed and produce validated, jurisdiction-ready product copy — with the brand manager at the gate, never replaced by it. Every decision logged, every rule citable, every variant traceable to its source.

How it works · end-to-end

Seven steps. One supplier feed in, three approved variants out.

Trace one product through the system — ProvAlim S.A. sends Maison Vellard a weekly Excel feed; one of the rows is the new Adult Sensitive Salmon & Rice kibble. That row becomes a France, a Netherlands and a Germany variant of approved product copy, with Camille Lefevre's signature on each one.

Company-profile vs legal asset split

A word on governance · before the walkthrough

The hardest question about AI here
isn't capability. It's governance.

A Shariah function carries fiduciary weight that doesn't compress. Before showing you what the system does, we want to name what we built it inside. Three commitments — the deck returns to each in detail at the end.

COMMITMENT 01
The SSC owns the thresholds.
Confidence thresholds and routing rules are Shariah risk-appetite parameters — not tech settings. The Shariah Supervisory Committee approves them. Engineering implements what they approved.
COMMITMENT 02
The silent majority is governed too.
The cases the system auto-clears don't disappear into volume. Edge-band review, statistical sampling, monthly Internal Shariah Audit (AAOIFI GS 11), and auto-suspend on drift. The SSC sees the population, not just the exceptions.
COMMITMENT 03
The model is replaceable. The governance isn't.
Model-agnostic by design — the SS1/23 P5 / ISO 42001 A.10 control for third-party model dependency. Six-layer hallucination control; manual FP/FN review by human testers establishes correctness; the Same-Outcome Quotient pipeline locks consistency on the tuned baseline; SSC approves the freeze.
We come back to this
Three dedicated slides at the end walk through threshold ownership, the silent-majority controls, and a worked AI Risk Register row — plus a regulatory map and a glossary in backup. For now: the walkthrough sits inside this frame.

Where the system intervenes.

Operational Sharia compliance across the transaction and restructuring lifecycle — not a business-activity halal/haram classifier.
A
Inquiry Resolution
Retail & private banking · front-line staff
Process
A customer or front-line officer asks whether a product or activity is Shariah-permissible. The question needs an answer: cited and consistent.
Pain
Different officers give different answers to the same question. Routine inquiries queue behind complex ones on the advisor's desk. No audit trail.
Solution
AI retrieves the applicable Board ruling and AAOIFI standard on submission. The desk officer reviews and endorses; novel cases reach the advisor pre-framed.
Value
Every inquiry answered the same way, every time, with a citation. The advisor handles only what requires genuine judgment.
B
Commercial Financing Clearance
SME & corporate financing · Relationship Managers
Process
An RM submits an SME or corporate financing application. Before credit, the deal must pass Shariah clearance across eight aspects.
Pain
Clearance takes 3–14 days. RM sometimes picks the wrong mode — deal fails at Shariah on Day 11. Doc gaps surface mid-process.
Solution
Correct mode recommended at intake. Eight aspects screened in parallel. Doc gaps named Day 1 with SME alternatives. Precedent cases cleared same morning.
Value
Most deals cleared the morning they're submitted. The RM calls the client with a decision — before the competing bank does.
C
Investment Portfolio Screening
Discretionary portfolios · Wealth Management
Process
Every Shariah-compliant portfolio is recertified each quarter against AAOIFI Standard 21 — every holding, every ratio.
Pain
24 holdings × 8 checks × manual pulls from 4 vendors = 19–22 days. By the time it clears, the data is a month old.
Solution
Overnight ingestion of new filings + market data. Ratios computed from parsed statements, every figure sourced. Clean holdings clear without the advisor seeing them.
Value
Three-week recertification becomes half-day exception review. The advisor sees the 3 flags, not the 24-holding spreadsheet.
D
Lifecycle Compliance In design
Post-financing surveillance · live book
Process
A 60-month facility lives long after drawdown: agent letters, fund flow, asset usage, breach detection, restructuring. Continuous, not annual.
Pain
Breaches surface 11 months late at audit. RMs reach for conventional rollovers (riba). Restructuring guessed, not designed.
Solution
Continuous attestation from core banking + telemetry. Severity-banded breaches. Restructuring engine surfaces Sharia-sound paths, blocks impermissible ones.
Value
Year-11-month surprises become Day-91 conversations. Restructuring becomes a Sharia-aware choice, not a credit gamble.

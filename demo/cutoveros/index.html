Solution Overview
CutoverOS

The intelligence layer for the D365 cutover lifecycle.

Plan elicitation, dependency intelligence, readiness scoring, consequence analysis, signal distillation, communications, and hypercare — across the highest-risk ten weeks of any D365 programme.

Nine modules across the cutover lifecycle

Three modules walked through in this session. The full system spans pre-cutover, go-live, and hypercare.

A
Cutover Plan Builder
Plan elicited through structured stakeholder interviews. Contradictions surfaced, durations benchmarked against prior cutovers.
·
Plan Intelligence
Live dependency graph, critical path, and schedule risk simulation against the benchmark library.
·
Readiness Scoring
Workstream-level readiness from objective signals — defect rates, migration runs, sign-offs — not from what gets reported.
B
Go/No-Go Consequence Engine
Every open item mapped to its plain-English business consequence. Severity-classified for steering committee decisions.
·
Communication Layer
Milestone-triggered stakeholder communications, audience-adapted for executive, functional, and end-user roles.
·
Integration Triggers
DevOps, LCS, and Teams sync. Task completions auto-release dependency locks downstream.
C
Cutover Signal Distiller
Continuous extraction of actions, risks, and decisions from email and meeting traffic. Drafted stakeholder communications ready to send.
·
Hypercare Triage
Inbound issue triage by severity. Pattern-matching against UAT defect history. Auto-answers for repeat questions.
·
Coaching & Decision Support
Programme coach. Burnout monitor. Decision-support prompts at go/no-go and other peak-pressure moments.
The Problem

300–600 tasks.
Dozens of owners.
One spreadsheet.

This is how every D365 cutover programme manages the most dangerous 72 hours of an implementation.

2–3 weeks
to build the plan — and it is still wrong
Workshop after workshop. Email after email. Assumptions dressed as validated facts. Structural gaps that only surface under pressure at T-minus 3 days — when there is no time to fix them.
3am
when the hidden dependency surfaces
A blocker arrives with four predecessor tasks still open. The dependency was always there. Nobody had mapped it. The plan looked complete because no one had asked the right cross-stakeholder question.
Green
in the steering deck. Amber in reality.
Programme managers filter the truth to manage upward anxiety. The steering committee makes a guess, not a decision. Nobody has a document that says what actually breaks if they go live today.

"These are not human failures. They are signal-processing failures.
CutoverOS closes that gap."

1
Severity classification
Each conflict is classified Blocker (gates plan assembly) or Manageable (proceeds with documented mitigation). Severity is derived from cross-workstream impact and downstream consequence, not from individual stakeholder judgement of urgency.
→ The steering committee sees a calibrated severity profile, not an unranked list.
2
Resolution paths preserve accountability
Three explicit paths: Resolve (with new validated information), Accept (with documented rationale), or Escalate (to a named decision-maker, with full context attached). Every action is audit-logged.
→ No conflict is silently dismissed; every outcome is traceable.
3
Source attribution preserved through resolution
Every conflict carries its source citations from detection through resolution. The Exflow blocker was raised from Anna's interview and the Charter document — both follow the conflict to the resolution screen, then to the audit log if accepted.
→ Every decision is defensible against any retrospective.

Critical path identification
Critical path tasks — the chain that determines go-live timing — are computed automatically and highlighted in red. Master data → reference → open transactions → integration tests → parallel run → go-live. Slip on any of these slips the cutover.
→ Schedule attention focuses on the tasks that actually matter.
2
Three-level hierarchy
Stage → Milestone → Task. Stages collapse for executive view, expand for operational detail. Milestones (◆) mark commitment points. Tasks carry owners, durations, and dependency links — no flat task list, ever.
→ One plan view serves both the steering deck and the cutover war room.
3
Plan stays in draft until conflicts close
The plan is generated and visible — but cannot be locked while three blockers remain unresolved in the Conflicts screen. The system never produces a "complete" plan that contains known structural contradictions.
→ The plan label always tells the truth about its readiness.

2
Quantified business impact

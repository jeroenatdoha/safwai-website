The Inflection Point

Why the consulting business model
is being disrupted — now

AI-native competitors are entering your market. Clients expect always-on access to expertise. The frameworks you have built over years can now be replicated by anyone with a prompt. This section sets out why the window to act is narrow — and what firms that move first gain.

Threat 1

Your methodology is one prompt away from replication

Generic AI tools let clients generate "good enough" frameworks from public knowledge. Without a proprietary platform encoding your specific methodology, depth, and context — your trademarked approach becomes a starting point anyone can approximate for free.

Threat 2

AI-native competitors don't charge by the day

New entrants with AI-native delivery models are undercutting project fees and offering always-on expertise. They have no consultant capacity constraints. They do not sleep, and they do not invoice by the workshop.

Threat 3

Clients expect continuous access, not periodic engagements

When a competitor offers a subscription platform delivering methodology 24/7 for a fixed monthly fee — the one-off workshop engagement becomes harder to justify at every renewal conversation. The client's reference point has changed.
